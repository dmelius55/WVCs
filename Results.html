How to Read These Hotspot Cards

Each card summarizes environmental and structural conditions at wildlife–vehicle collision hotspot locations in New Hampshire. These assessments help prioritize which crossings need improvements most urgently.

🎯 Priority Scoring: Each site is scored on four criteria. Lower total scores indicate worse conditions and higher priority for intervention. The worst possible score is 4 (all red dots), the best is 12 (all green dots).

Colored dots show current conditions and points assigned:

🟢 Green (3 points): Conditions are good and closely match natural stream and wildlife needs.
🟡 Yellow (2 points): Conditions are functional but partially limiting.
🔴 Red (1 point): Conditions strongly limit wildlife movement or natural stream processes.

Four assessment criteria:

💧 Aquatic Organism Passage (AOP) Ability for fish and aquatic species to move through the structure.
🛠️ Structural Condition (SC) Physical condition and integrity of the culvert or crossing.
🌍 Geomorphic Compatibility (GC) How well the structure matches natural stream shape and flow patterns.
📏 Openness (Open) Ratio of crossing dimensions that affects wildlife willingness to pass through.
